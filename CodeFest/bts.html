Week 1 | Getting Started

During the first week, we sorted out our ideas on a Google Doc and used WhatsApp to start a group chat. Later on, we switched over to Discord to communicate because Discord has a group call and screen sharing feature.

Preparation:

  • Set up a UnityID, UnityHub, and Unity Version 2019.4.17f1
  • Look for and/or create assets
  • Look up and follow online tutorials

Our Intial Ideas:

  • Game about epidemics and contact tracing
  • Target Audience: General Public
  • Point and Click Style game
  • First Person Perspective
  • Need concrete objectives
  • End Goal: Limit the spread of the virus using Covid-19 data
  • The player clicks on textboxes to make a decision
  • When the player makes a choice, there will be a popup that will explain why the selected choice was good or bad
  • The player's choices affect future events
  • The player can play as a person in power

Week 2 | Visuals and Gameplay

During the second week, we focused on the gameplay and what the player's screen would look like. We also assigned ourselves roles depending on our experience. We have 6 members, but the Unity Student Plan has a maximium of 5 seats. Therefore, one person must be excluded from the Unity part of the project. Unfortunately, the person in charge of the website, Clare, would not have access to the Unity project. But this is the most efficient arrangement because since Clare is in charge of the website, she does not need access to the game to make the website. Although we had different roles, we still continued to communicate on group calls and on the group chat.

Ideas:

  • Point and Click
  • Player will play as a journalist
  • Player Perspective: Side of the Room | First Person | 2D
  • Pixel Art Style
  • Mouse Input only
  • Include an inventory system
  • Aspect Ratio: 16:9 | Background Image: 300x200 pixels
  • Include infecton chance percentage
  • Could possibly shift to a Visual Novel Style game
